Issues

Prevent Burn from Cooking

Visually impact people are not able to see the hot objects in kitchen, so they might get burnt when they cook.

Cook Safely

Allowing the visually impact people to cook safely at home as the temperature sensor from thewill alert them if they are too close to a hot object.

Increase Surroundings Awareness

Visually impact people who wear bracelets would be able to know more about their surroundings and prevent injuries.

Solutions

Innovative ideas to help visually impact people to protect themselves.

We came out an idea with a bracelet that would help them stay away from hot objects and to ensure their safty

  • Functionality

    How does it work

    The bracelet is a wearable device. It will detect the temperature of the warm objects. If the temperature of the hot object is higher than 80 degree and the distance is closer to visually impact people, it will alert them with high pitch sound and vibration.

  • Usability

    How should they use it

    Visually impact people will wear it all the time. When they approach hot objects, they would alert by the sound and vibration from the bracelet.

  • Impact

    How can they benefit

    Visually impact people can move freely at home without worrying about getting burnt or injuried.

Beneficiary

Who are our target group

Blindness is a lack of vision, also known as visual impairment. It may also refer to a loss of vision that cannot be corrected with glasses or contact lenses. Three-quarters of all blindness can be prevented or treated. Partial blindness means that the person has very limited vision. Complete blindness means that the person cannot see anything or has no perception of light. People with a vision worse than 20/200 are considered legally blind.

Causes

What cause blindness

  • Diabetes
  • Age-related macular degeneration
  • Accidents (such as chemical burns or sports injuries)
  • Glaucoma
  • Cataracts
  • Trachoma
  • Leprosy
  • Vitamin A deficiency

Statistics

Number of visually impact people

The estimated number of people visually impaired in the world stands at 285 million, with 39 million diagnosed blind. Asia alone accounts for 63.4% and 57.5% of worldwide visual impairment (VI) and blindness, respectively.
