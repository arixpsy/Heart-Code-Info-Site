Traffic

Colour blind people face prombles driving a car a they have to look out for light signals and traffic light they can get very frustrated with electrical goods which have red/green/orange LED displays



Food

Colour blind people can face prombles when they are choosing food or preparing them.Some food can look repulsive if you are colour blind, and colour blind children can seem particularly fussy over green vegetables – spinach can look like cow pat and colour blind children probably mean it if they say their food looks like poo!



Vegetables

Most red/green colour blind people won’t know if they’ve cooked a piece of meat rare or well done and they’re unlikely to be able to tell the difference between green and ripe tomatoes or between ketchup and chocolate sauce. Colour blind people often try to eat unripe bananas because they can’t tell the difference between a green unripe banana and a yellow ripe banana – to them because both of the colours are the same shade they often think they are the same colour.



Clothes

Colour blind people can even face problems when they are selecting clothes to wear.



Mood

Colour blind people can also find themselves in trouble because they haven’t been able to pick up a change in someone’s mood by a change in colour of their face, or not noticed their child getting sunburnt.



More Information about Colour Blindness

Color blindness occurs when light-sensitive cells in the retina fail to respond appropriately to variations in wavelengths of light that enable people to see an array of colors. in the retina are called rods and cones.
